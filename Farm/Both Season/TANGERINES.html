TANGERINE

Description: Tangerines (Citrus reticulata) are small, easy-to- peel citrus fruits with sweet, slightly tart flavors. Known for their bright orange skin and juicy segments, they are rich in vitamin C, dietary fiber, and antioxidants. Tangerines are widely enjoyed fresh or used in juices, salads, and desserts. These trees are medium-sized and thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Spacing:4 to 6 meters between plants; 6 meters between rows.

Best Soil Type/Condition:Well-drained sandy loam soils rich in organic matter with pH value ranging from 5.5 to 6.5.

Growth Time:3 to 4 years from transplanting to their first harvest. Once mature, they can produce fruits annually.

Water Rate: Require consistent watering. 1 to 2 inches depth of water per week; avoid overwatering. Deep watering every 7 to 10 days during the dry season.

Fertilization: Apply a balanced fertilizer at planting and during growth phases. Apply 1 to 2 kg of fertilizer per tree per year, divided into 2-3 applications (e.g., at the start of the growing season, midseason, and post-harvest). Additionally, supplement with micronutrients like magnesium, zinc, and manganese to enhance fruit quality. Incorporate organic matter like compost to improve soil health.
