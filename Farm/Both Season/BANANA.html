BANANA

Description: Bananas, from the genus Musa, are one of the world’s most important fruit crops, valued for their flavor, nutrition, and year-round availability. Cavendish bananas are typically eaten fresh but are also used in desserts like pies, muffins, and breads. Plantains (saba), a starchy variety, serve as a staple food in tropical regions and are usually cooked. Rich in carbohydrates, fiber, potassium, and vitamins B6 and C, bananas are both delicious and nutritious. There are more than 12 varieties of bananas in the Philippines. Some of the popular ones include Lakatan, Latundan, Saba, and Cavendish.

Spacing: 1.8 x 1.8 meters between plants.

Best Soil Type/Condition:Well-drained, fertile soil rich in organic matter with pH value ranging from 5.5 to 6.5.

Growth Time: 9-12 months from planting to harvesting.

Water Rate: Water every 2 to 3 days to keep soil moist but not waterlogged.

Fertilization: Apply ½ cup of balanced fertilizer with micronutrients at planting; fertilize 4–6 times per year as the plant grows.
