EGGPLANT

Description: Eggplant is a staple vegetable valued for its versatility and nutrition. It is rich in potassium, iron, protein, and vitamins A and B, while being low in calories and fat. Packet with antioxidants, eggplant supports heart and brain health and plays an important role in agriculture as a reliable and nutritious crop.

Spacing: 45cm to 60cm between plants; 75 to 90cm between rows.

Best Soil Type/Condition: Eggplants thrive best in sandy loam and clay loam textured soils with pH value ranging from 5.5 to 6.8.

Growth Time: 65 to 80 days from transplanting to harvest. 100 to 120 days from seed to harvest.

Water Rate: 2.5 to 5 cm depth of water per week.

Fertilization: Pre-planting fertilization incorporates 2 to 3 pounds of complete fertilizer per 100 square meters into the soil. Post-planting requires side-dress nitrogen-rich fertilizer at 4 and 8 weeks after planting.
