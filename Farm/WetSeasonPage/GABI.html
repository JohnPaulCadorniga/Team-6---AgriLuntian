GABI

Description: Gabi (taro) is a root crop cultivated for its edible corms, leaves, and petioles. It is easily recognizable by its large, heart-shaped leaves and is a staple ingredient in Filipino dishes such as laing and sinigang. Gabi is rich in essential nutrients, including dietary fiber, vitamin C, vitamin E, potassium, and manganese. Its corms are a good source of complex carbohydrates, providing long-lasting energy, while its leaves are high in iron and antioxidants. This crop promotes digestive health, boosts immunity, and supports heart health

Spacing: 30 to 60 cm between plants; 60 to 90 cm between rows.

Best Soil Type/Condition: Loamy or silty soils rich in organic matter with pH value ranging from 5.5 to 7.0.s

Growth Time: 6 to 8 months from planting to harvest.

Water Rate: 5 to 20 cm flooding depth of water.

Fertilization: Use balanced NPK fertilizer (14-14-14) at planting; side-dress with nitrogen fertilizer (urea) 45 to 60 days after planting.
